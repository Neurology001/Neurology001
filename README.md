## Positioning

I work at the intersection of **neurology**, **clinical reasoning**, and **deployable medical software**. My focus is not only to build models, but to turn clinical prediction research into tools that can be inspected, explained, deployed, and improved in real workflows.

> Calm interface. Transparent model logic. Clinically accountable software.

## Current Focus

- Translating neurology prediction research into usable software rather than static notebook results.
- Building specialty-disease data systems that preserve clinical meaning, data quality, and extensibility.
- Improving medical AI outputs with calibration, interpretability, deployment packaging, and plain clinical review paths.
- Designing interfaces that reduce cognitive load for clinicians instead of adding another layer of noise.

## Working Principles

<table>
  <tr>
    <td width="25%"><strong>Localize</strong><br />Start from the clinical problem and the decision being supported.</td>
    <td width="25%"><strong>Instrument</strong><br />Expose assumptions, field definitions, model behavior, and failure modes.</td>
    <td width="25%"><strong>Validate</strong><br />Report discrimination, calibration, clinical utility, and leakage controls.</td>
    <td width="25%"><strong>Ship</strong><br />Make the runtime artifact match the source intent, not just the codebase.</td>
  </tr>
</table>
